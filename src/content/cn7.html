Facial nerve (CN 7)

Anatomy & function

  • Starts in the Pons
  • Travels through the facial canal, close to CN 8
  • Exits through the stylomastoid foramen
  • Innervates muscles of facial expression
  • Allows us to furrow our forehead, close our eyes and blink, smile, frown, etc.
  • Parasympathetic innervation to lacrimal, nasal, palatine, and salivary glands

Facial nerve palsy

  • Bell's palsy (inflammation)
  • Lyme disease
  • Nerve blocks and neuropathies
  • Stroke
  • Fracture
  • Autoimmune disorders
  • Cancer
  • May lead to hyperacusis if affecting tensor tympani & stapedius

Assessment

RED FLAGS

  • Limb paresis
  • Limb or facial paresthesia
  • Trauma
  • Signs of cancer (slow onset, pain, ipsilateral hearing loss)
  • Acute infections

Hemifacial paresis

  • During conversation, grimacing, smiling, etc.

UMN or LMN?

  • Is eye closure and forehead furrowing preserved?
  • Are there associated signs of vertigo, ataxia, hyperreflexia, limb paresis?
  • To distinguish between brainstem and upper cortical centers, check for contralateral limb involvement involuntary movements of the face
  • If UMN lesion is excluded assess the eyes, ears, mastoid region, oral cavity, scalp, and glands

Taste

  • Assess both sides of the anterior 2/3 of tongue
  • Use a cotton swab with sweet, bitter, sour, and salty solutions

Hearing

  • Use a vibrating tuning fork

Take into consideration

  • Partial paralysis has a better prognosis than complete paralysis
  • Medical management comes first; ensure patient adherence
